Most people fail to change because they focus on the wrong thing. They focus on outcomes (losing 20 pounds) rather than the process (walking every day). Clear introduces the concept of Identity-Based Habits, suggesting that the most effective way to change your behavior is to focus not on what you want to achieve, but on who you wish to become. Every action you take is a "vote" for the type of person you want to be Clear also addresses the "Plateau of Latent Potential"—the frustrating period where you put in work but see no results.
